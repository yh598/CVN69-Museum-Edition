# Glue-only air-wing assembly

1. Print `Print_Plate_00_First_Article.3mf` at 100% before committing the full air wing. Record the physical result; automated PASS is not a substitute.
2. Print aircraft bodies in Basic Blue Grey. Allow them to cool before clipping the removable 0.80 mm belly/engine support rails. Sand only the rail witness; do not thin wings, fins, rotors, or landing supports.
3. Dry-fit the Charcoal canopy, Ivory rotodome/identity, and Silver rotor objects. Correct flash or rail remnants—never scale the mating parts.
4. Apply a pinhead amount of medium or thick CA to the flat seat. Capillary thin CA can fog canopies and flood 0.30 mm raised details.
5. Use the selected layout JSON and `Docs/AirWing_Layout_Guide.pdf`. Maintain 0.20 mm per-side aircraft clearance and keep island, weapon, elevator, catapult, wire, and raised-marking exclusion geometry clear.
6. Glue aircraft only after the hull, deck, island, and defensive systems are fully cured. The integrated STEP/3MF files are non-production placement reviews.

No magnets, metal pins, screws, wire, or purchased connectors are required or approved. Do not modify the frozen hull/deck interface or any approved Milestone 1–4 geometry.
