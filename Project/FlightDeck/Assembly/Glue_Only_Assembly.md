# CVN-69 Flight Deck — Glue-Only Assembly

## Print order

1. Print one small elevator and its perimeter marking as a detail-quality test.
2. Print `Print_Plate_01_Main_Deck.3mf` flat with no support.
3. Print `Print_Plate_02_Details.3mf`; use a brim for the longest 0.50–0.80 mm wide strips if your bed adhesion is marginal.
4. Confirm every part is flat and dimensionally unchanged before gluing.

## Main deck

1. Identify modules from stern to bow: Module 1 (x = 0–190 mm plus tongues), Module 2 (x = 190–330 mm plus tongues), Module 3 (x = 330–476 mm).
2. Dry-fit the two 7 mm underside tongues at each seam. The female sockets provide 0.25 mm clearance per side.
3. Align all modules top-side down on a verified flat surface. The deck edges and top faces must be flush.
4. Apply a small amount of medium or gel CA to the tongues and butt faces. Join one seam at a time and let it stabilize before moving to the next seam.

## Elevators

Each 1.8 mm thick elevator plate seats on a 1.2 mm continuous shelf. Dry-fit before adhesive; the plate top should be flush with the 3.0 mm deck surface. Apply adhesive sparingly to the shelf, not the visible perimeter.

## Raised details

Install the four catapult tracks, four arresting wires, landing-area marking parts, bow centerline, and elevator perimeter frames only after the deck modules and elevator plates are fixed. Use the top render as the placement map. The details are 0.35–0.40 mm high and should not be forced into position.

## Material note

The FCStd and 3MF use charcoal for the deck, gray for elevator plates, white for markings, silver for catapult tracks, and yellow for arresting wires. These assignments are visual/material suggestions, not required for geometry.
