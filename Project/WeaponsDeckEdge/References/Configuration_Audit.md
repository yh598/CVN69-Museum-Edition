# CVN-69 Defensive Systems and Deck-Edge Configuration Audit

Access date for all web references: **2026-07-14**.

## Frozen configuration and evidence rule

This reconstruction is frozen to USS *Dwight D. Eisenhower*'s **2023-10-14 through 2024-07-14 deployment**, with the visible fit anchored to official imagery dated **2024-06-30** and the unobstructed port-side return sequence dated **2024-07-14**. It is a public, unclassified, photo-derived display reconstruction—not a shipyard drawing and not controlled technical data.

The official 2019 CVN-69 combat-systems article confirms that the ship operated Phalanx CIWS, Rolling Airframe Missile, and rearchitectured NATO Sea Sparrow systems. Official 2024 aerial, bow-quarter, head-on, and port-side photographs were then cross-read to identify visible platform silhouettes and to avoid importing an incompatible older carrier fit. The modeled total is **two Mk 15 Phalanx, two Mk 49 RAM, and two Mk 29 Sea Sparrow/ESSM installations**. No single public photograph exposes all six unobstructed; the count is therefore a cross-image interpretation and is labeled medium rather than shipyard-verified confidence.

## Differences from earlier fits and omissions

- Older imagery is not used to add extra mounts or to change the Milestone 3 radar/island fit. The 2025 planned incremental availability and all later maintenance imagery are outside the freeze.
- No additional gun mounts, small arms, directors, electronic-warfare boxes, cable runs, ammunition, internal mechanisms, or speculative antenna fits are modeled. Public evidence was insufficient to distinguish them reliably at 1:700 or they belong to the approved island.
- Mk 49 and Mk 29 cell-face relief is deliberately enlarged and reduced in cell count for 0.4 mm-nozzle reliability; it is not dimensionally exact.
- The utility boat is intentionally generic because the public photographs do not establish a reliable subtype. It is not claimed to be a shipyard-accurate RHIB.
- No weapon is functional. Launchers do not elevate or rotate; the CIWS barrel is a fixed, enlarged display representation.

No classified, restricted, leaked, controlled, private-maintenance, or unverifiable social-media material was used.
